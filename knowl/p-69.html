We now give a criterion for a degree \((1,n)\) RISP to possess parabolic fixed points in terms of the one-variable polynomial \(Q_{\alpha}\text{.}\) As in (3.2) in SectionÂ 3, we write \(\phi=e^{i\alpha}\frac{\tilde{p}}{p}\text{,}\) where

\begin{equation*} p(z)=p_1(z_2)+z_1p_2(z_2), \end{equation*}

and \(\tilde{p}(z)=\tilde{p}_2(z_2)+z_1\tilde{p}_1(z_2)\) for a pair of one-variable polynomials \(p_1,p_2\text{.}\) Recall that

\begin{equation*} Q_{\alpha}=(p_1-e^{i\alpha}\tilde{p}_1)^2+4e^{i\alpha}p_2\tilde{p}_2. \end{equation*}

We shall need the auxiliary expressions

\begin{equation} \psi^{1}_{\alpha}(z_2)=\frac{1}{2p_2(z_2)}\left(p_1(z_2)-e^{i\alpha}\tilde{p}_1(z_2)+\sqrt{Q_{\alpha}(z_2)}\right)\tag{4.3} \end{equation}

and

\begin{equation} \psi^{2}_{\alpha}(z_2)=\frac{1}{2p_2(z_2)}\left(p_1(z_2)-e^{i\alpha}\tilde{p}_1(z_2)-\sqrt{Q_{\alpha}(z_2)}\right).\tag{4.4} \end{equation}

The functions \(\psi^j_{\alpha}\) parametrize the roots of the polynomial

\begin{equation*} P_ {\alpha}(z)=e^{i\alpha}\tilde{p}-z_1p=e^{i\alpha}\tilde{p}_2+e^{i\alpha}z_1\tilde{p}_1-z_1p_1-z_1^2p_2 \end{equation*}

away from the set \(\Lambda^{\sharp}=\{z_2\in \mathbb{C}\colon p_2(z_2)=0\}\text{.}\) Note that \(\Lambda^{\flat}\cap \Lambda^{\sharp}=\emptyset\) since \(p_1(z_2)\neq 0\) for \(z_2\in \overline{\mathbb{D}}\text{.}\)
