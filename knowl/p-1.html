We examine iteration of certain skew-products on the bidisk whose components are rational inner functions, with emphasis on simple maps of the form \(\Phi(z_1,z_2) = (\phi(z_1,z_2), z_2)\text{.}\) If \(\phi\) has degree \(1\) in the first variable, the dynamics on each horizontal fiber can be described in terms of MÃ¶bius transformations but the global dynamics on the \(2\)-torus exhibit some complexity, encoded in terms of certain \(\mathbb{T}^2\)-symmetric polynomials. We describe the dynamical behavior of such mappings \(\Phi\) and give criteria for different configurations of fixed point curves and rotation belts in terms of zeros of a related one-variable polynomial.
