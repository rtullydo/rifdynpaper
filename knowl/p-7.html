In contrast with one-variable Blaschke products, a general rational inner function \(\psi=q/p\) in \(\mathbb{D}^2\) can have boundary singularities: these occur at points \(\tau=(\tau_1,\tau_2)\in \mathbb{T}^2\) where \(q(\tau_1,\tau_2)=p(\tau_1,\tau_2)=0\text{,}\) and represent a genuinely new higher-dimensional phenomenon. The function \(\psi\) is in general discontinuous at \(\tau \in \mathbb{T}^2\text{,}\) even though \(\psi^{*}(\tau)\) always exists, meaning that a RISP \(\Phi\) need not be a continuous self-map of \(\overline{\mathbb{D}^2}\text{.}\) This fact will be the source of several interesting phenomena that we observe in this paper.
