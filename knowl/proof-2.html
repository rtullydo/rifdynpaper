Proof

This amounts to a computation. Applying the reflection operation (2.2) to the polynomial \(P_1\) and exploiting the fact that reflection is an involution, we obtain

\begin{gather*} \tilde{P}_1(z_1,z_2)=z_1^{n_1+1}z_2^{n_2}e^{-i\alpha_1}\cc{\tilde{p}}_1\left(\frac{1}{\cc{z}_1}, \frac{1}{\cc{z}_2}\right)-z_1^{n_1+1}z_2^{n_2}\frac{1}{z_1}\cc{p}_1\left(\frac{1}{\cc{z}_1}, \frac{1}{\cc{z}_2}\right)\\ =e^{-i\alpha_1}z_1p_1(z_1,z_2)-\tilde{p}_1(z_1,z_2)=-e^{-i\alpha_1}(-z_1p_1(z_1,z_2)+e^{i\alpha_1}\tilde{p}_1(z_1,z_2))\\=-e^{-i\alpha_1}P_1. \end{gather*}

A similar computation establishes the assertion for \(P_2\text{.}\)
