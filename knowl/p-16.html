Our next example is

\begin{equation*} \Phi(z_1,z_2)=\left(-\frac{3z_1z_2-z_1-z_2-1}{3-z_1-z_2-z_1z_2},z_2\right). \end{equation*}

The first component map \(\phi=-\frac{\tilde{p}}{p}\) again has a unique singularity on \(\mathbb{T}^2\) at the point \((1,1)\text{,}\) with \(\phi^*(1,1)=1\text{,}\) and since \(\Phi(z_1,1)=(1,1)\text{,}\) the fiber \(\{z_2=1\}\) collapses. The fixed points of \(\Phi\) on the \(2\)-torus are determined by \(\tilde{p}-z_1p=0\text{,}\) and since \(\tilde{p}-z_1p=-(z_1-1)^2(z_2+1)\text{,}\) the fixed point consists of \(\{z_1=1\}\cup\{z_2=-1\}\text{,}\) a union of two lines.
