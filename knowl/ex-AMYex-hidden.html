Consider the rational inner function

\begin{equation} \phi(z_1,z_2)=-\frac{\tilde{p}(z)}{p(z)}=-\frac{4z_1z_2^2 - z_2^2 - 3z_1z_2 - z_2 + z_1}{4 - z_1 - 3z_2 - z_1z_2 + z_2^2},\tag{5.1} \end{equation}

which features as an example in the paper [3], and has been further studied in [9][Section 1.3] and [6][Example 5.2] (without the minus sign in front). Since \(\phi\) has a single singularity at \((1,1)\) and \(\phi^*(1,1)=1\text{,}\) the corresponding RISP \(\Phi\) has a SF-point at \((1,1)\text{.}\) We further check that \(\phi(1,z_2)=1\text{,}\) confirming the presence of a collapsing fiber.

In this example, \(\alpha=\pi\text{,}\) and

\begin{equation*} p(z)=p_1(z_2)+z_1p_2(z_2) \end{equation*}

with

\begin{equation*} p_1(z_2)=4-3z_2+z_2^2\quad \textrm{and}\quad p_2(z_2)=-(1+z_2). \end{equation*}

We have \(p_2(-1)=0\text{,}\) and as expected \(\phi(z_1,-1)=-z_1\text{,}\) a rotation of order \(2\text{.}\) A short computation shows that the associated polynomial \(Q_{\alpha}\) is given by

\begin{equation} Q_{\alpha}(z_2)=25(z_2-1)^2\left(z_2^2-\frac{14}{25}z_2+1\right).\tag{5.2} \end{equation}

Then \(Q_{\alpha}(z_2)\) has a double root at \(\lambda_1=1\text{,}\) and two roots at \(\lambda_{2,3}=\frac{1}{25}(7\pm 24i)\text{.}\)

Figure 5.2 displays the dynamics of \(\Phi\) on \(\mathbb{T}^2\text{.}\) We notice a single rotation belt bounded by \(F_{\lambda_2}\) and \(F_{\lambda_3}\text{,}\) one fewer than the maximum given the degree of \(Q_{\alpha}\text{.}\) Also visible are two curves containing hyperbolic fixed points with a normal crossing at \((1,1)\text{,}\) which is contained in the collapsing fiber.

The hyperbolic fixed points are visible on a pair of curves parametrized by

\begin{equation*} \psi^{1,2}_{-1}(z_2)=\frac{1}{2(1+z_2)}\left(5 - 6 z_2 +5 z_2^2 \pm (-1 + z_2) \sqrt{25 - 14 z_2 + 25 z_2^2}\right). \end{equation*}

These curves exhibit a normal crossing at \((1,1)\text{,}\) the singularity of the RIF, reflecting the double root of \(Q_{\alpha}\) at \((1,1)\text{.}\) The branch point nature of the parabolic fixed points at \(\lambda_2\) and \(\lambda_3\) can be observed in Figure 5.3 which displays the absolute value of the functions \(\psi^{1,2}(e^{it_2})\text{.}\)
