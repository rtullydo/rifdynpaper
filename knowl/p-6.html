Iteration of certain classes of rational inner functions on the bidisk and on \(\mathbb{T}^2\) has been considered in several papers, for instance in [23, 22]; see also [26] for some applications. In those works, the component maps \(\phi_j\) are assumed to be of the special type

\begin{equation*} \psi_j(z_1,z_2)=B_{j,1}(z_1)\cdot B_{j,2}(z_2) \end{equation*}

where \(B_{j,k}\) are one-variable finite Blaschke products. Recall that these are functions \(B\colon \mathbb{D}\to\mathbb{D}\) of the form

\begin{equation*} B(z)=e^{i\alpha}z^m\prod_{k=1}^n\frac{z-a_k}{1-\overline{a}_kz}, \end{equation*}

where \(\{a_1,\ldots, a_n\}\subset \mathbb{D}\text{,}\) \(\alpha\in \mathbb{R}\text{,}\) and \(m,n\in \mathbb{N}\text{.}\) It is apparent that a finite Blaschke product extends continuously to the unit circle \(\mathbb{T}\text{,}\) but the dynamics of one-variable Blaschke products nevertheless exhibit complicated features; see for instance [19] Chapter 15. Similarly, the works [23, 22] uncover a rich dynamical structure associated with two-dimensional Blaschke products.
