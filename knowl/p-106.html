Consider the rational inner function

\begin{equation} \phi(z_1,z_2)=-\frac{\tilde{p}(z)}{p(z)}=-\frac{4z_1z_2^2 - z_2^2 - 3z_1z_2 - z_2 + z_1}{4 - z_1 - 3z_2 - z_1z_2 + z_2^2},\tag{5.1} \end{equation}

which features as an example in the paper [3], and has been further studied in [9][Section 1.3] and [6][Example 5.2] (without the minus sign in front). Since \(\phi\) has a single singularity at \((1,1)\) and \(\phi^*(1,1)=1\text{,}\) the corresponding RISP \(\Phi\) has a SF-point at \((1,1)\text{.}\) We further check that \(\phi(1,z_2)=1\text{,}\) confirming the presence of a collapsing fiber.
