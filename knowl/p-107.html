In this example, \(\alpha=\pi\text{,}\) and

\begin{equation*} p(z)=p_1(z_2)+z_1p_2(z_2) \end{equation*}

with

\begin{equation*} p_1(z_2)=4-3z_2+z_2^2\quad \textrm{and}\quad p_2(z_2)=-(1+z_2). \end{equation*}

We have \(p_2(-1)=0\text{,}\) and as expected \(\phi(z_1,-1)=-z_1\text{,}\) a rotation of order \(2\text{.}\) A short computation shows that the associated polynomial \(Q_{\alpha}\) is given by

\begin{equation} Q_{\alpha}(z_2)=25(z_2-1)^2\left(z_2^2-\frac{14}{25}z_2+1\right).\tag{5.2} \end{equation}

Then \(Q_{\alpha}(z_2)\) has a double root at \(\lambda_1=1\text{,}\) and two roots at \(\lambda_{2,3}=\frac{1}{25}(7\pm 24i)\text{.}\)
