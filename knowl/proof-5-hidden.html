By the definition of the reflection operation, \(p_2(\lambda)=0\) implies \(\tilde{p}_2(\lambda)=0\text{.}\) Next, if also \(Q_{\alpha}(\lambda)=0\) then \((p_1(\lambda)-e^{i\alpha}\tilde{p}_1(\lambda))^2=0\) so that \(p_1( \lambda)=e^{i\alpha}\tilde{p}_1(\lambda)\text{.}\) But then

\begin{equation*} \phi(z_1,\lambda)=\frac{z_1\tilde{p}_1(\lambda)+0}{p_1(\lambda)+0}=e^{-i\alpha}z_1. \end{equation*}

Conversely, suppose

\begin{equation*} e^{-i\alpha}z_1=\frac{z_1\tilde{p}_1(\lambda)+\tilde{p}_2(\lambda)}{p_1(\lambda)+p_2(\lambda)z_1} \end{equation*}

for some unimodular \(\lambda\text{.}\) Then, comparing coefficients, we get \(p_2(\lambda)=0\) and as a direct consequence that \(\tilde{p}_2(\lambda)=0\text{.}\) Finally, we read off that \(e^{-i\alpha}=\frac{\tilde{p}_1(\lambda)}{p_1(\lambda)}\) and hence \(Q_{\alpha}(\lambda)=0\text{,}\) as claimed.
