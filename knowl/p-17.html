Since \(\phi(z_1,-1)=z_1\text{,}\) we obtain \(\Phi(z_1,-1)=(z_1,-1)\text{,}\) confirming that all the points on \(\{z_2=-1\}\) are fixed. By contrast, all other fibers contain a single fixed point at \((1,z_2)\text{.}\) The dynamics of \(\Phi\) are shown in FigureÂ 2.4: we see that when \(t_2>0\text{,}\) points are attracted to the \(t_2\)-axis from the right, and when \(t_2\lt 0\text{,}\) the fibers are attracted to the axis from the left. A concrete formula for \(\lim_{n\to \infty}\Phi\) is not readily apparent, but a computation shows that

\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial z_1}(z_1,z_2)=4\frac{(z_2-1)^2}{(3-z_1-z_2-z_1z_2)^2}, \end{equation*}

hence \(\frac{\partial \phi}{\partial z_1}(1,z_2)=1\text{.}\) As we will see later, this confirms that the fixed points on \(\{(1,\zeta_2)\}\) are neither attractive nor repelling in this example: they are so-called parabolic fixed points.
