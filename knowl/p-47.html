Consider a bidegree \((n_1,n_2)\) rational inner mapping \(\Phi=(\phi_1, \phi_2)\text{,}\) where \(\phi_j=e^{i\alpha_j}\frac{\tilde{p}_j}{p_j}\text{,}\) \(j=1,2\text{,}\) and where as usual each \(p_j \in \mathbb{C}[z_1,z_2]\) has no zeros in \(\mathbb{D}\text{.}\) The set of fixed points of \(\Phi\) in \(\cc{\mathbb{D}^2}\) consists of all \(z \in \overline{\mathbb{D}^2}\) such that

\begin{equation*} \Phi(z_1,z_2)=(z_1,z_2); \end{equation*}

recall that \(\Phi\) is well-defined at each \(\eta \in \mathbb{T}^2\) [18][Corollary 14.6].
